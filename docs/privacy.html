Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 8, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how TripTalk ("the App"), developed by Michael Oehlrich ("we," "us," or "our"), handles information when you use our iOS application.

Summary

TripTalk does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data. The App operates entirely on your device with no server-side component, no user accounts, and no analytics.

Information We Do Not Collect

We do not collect any of the following:

  • Personal identification information (name, email, phone number)
  • Location data
  • Device identifiers or fingerprints
  • Usage analytics or behavioral data
  • Health or biometric data
  • Financial or payment information

Local Data Storage

All content you create within TripTalk — including experience reports, reviews, notes, and preferences — is stored exclusively on your device using local storage. This data is never transmitted to our servers or any third party, because we do not operate servers or use third-party services.

Third-Party Services

TripTalk does not integrate any third-party analytics, advertising, crash reporting, or tracking frameworks. No data is shared with third parties.

Cookies and Tracking

TripTalk is a native iOS application and does not use cookies, web beacons, pixels, or any other tracking technologies.

Children's Privacy

TripTalk is rated 17+ on the App Store and implements an additional 21+ age verification gate within the app. The App is not intended for use by anyone under the age of 21. We do not knowingly collect information from children or minors.

California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)

Because TripTalk does not collect, sell, or share personal information, the rights granted under the CCPA — including the right to know, delete, and opt out of sale — are satisfied by default. There is no personal information to disclose, delete, or restrict.

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)

TripTalk does not process personal data of any users, including those in the European Economic Area. No data controller or processor obligations arise because no personal data is collected or processed.

Data Security

Since all data remains on your device, the security of your information is governed by your device's built-in security features, including device passcode, Face ID, or Touch ID. We recommend keeping your device software up to date.

Changes to This Policy

If we update this Privacy Policy, the revised version will be posted on this page with an updated "Last updated" date. We encourage you to review this page periodically. Continued use of the App after changes constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
